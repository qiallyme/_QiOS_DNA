# Quick tips for consulate use (short)

- Print on official letterhead if possible (adds weight).
    
- Attach photocopies of both spouses' passports, IDs, and any prior civil documents requested by the consulate.
    
- Attach a copy of the officiant’s credential or proof of authority to solemnize marriages.
    
- Make sure the affidavit is notarized and includes the notary seal — consulates often require notarized affidavits.
    
- If the consulate requires an apostille or legalisation for the country of issuance, complete that step before submission. (If you want I can draft the apostille cover text next.)
